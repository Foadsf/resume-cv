\section{Summary}
I invent robot hardware and make it real. For my PhD I designed a new class of pneumatic stepper actuator: fully 3D-printed, electromagnetically stealth, 850 RPM, with no metal, no electronics, and no lubrication, and safe to run inside an MRI scanner (two patents, IEEE ICRA and RA-L, featured on Hackaday). Since 2020 I have worked at the sharp end of precision manufacturing for ASML's machines (VDL ETG, then NTS), building the tooling and hardware-in-the-loop rigs that turn the hardest mechanical designs into buildable, validated hardware. Hands-on across the whole stack: mechanism, actuation, sensing, control, test, and manufacture.
